The Bible & Revelation

    How do we know the Bible wasn’t altered by humans?

    Many religious adherents claim their scriptures are divinely preserved. Yet historical evidence suggests changes, translations, and omissions. Can divine truth be entrusted to imperfect scribes?

    Why are there contradictions in scripture?

    If the Bible is the infallible word of God, why do we find inconsistencies in timelines, genealogies, and moral teachings across its books? Are these signs of human hands or divine paradox?

God’s Nature

    How can God have emotions if He is perfect and unchanging?

    Emotions imply change and reaction. If God is immutable, how can He feel anger, regret, or love without being influenced by creation?

    Why would an omnipotent being need worship?

    Does an all-powerful, self-sufficient deity desire or demand praise for its own sake—or is worship for the worshipper’s benefit? And if so, why make it mandatory?

Faith vs. Evidence

  1. Why does God require faith instead of evidence?
  2. If faith is key, why do some believe so easily while others struggle?
  3. Why are those who question or doubt punished while those who blindly believe are rewarded?
  4. Can faith be a reliable path to truth if every religion uses it to justify opposing beliefs?
  5. If God wants a relationship, why remain hidden and communicate through ancient texts?
  6. Why are miracles and divine communication absent in modern, well-documented eras?
  7. Why does God allow so much religious confusion if salvation depends on correct belief?
  8. Why are critical thinkers or skeptics often portrayed as evil or rebellious in scripture?
  9. Shouldn't a just God welcome honest inquiry instead of punishing doubt?
  10. If the truth will set us free, why discourage asking hard questions about God?
  11. Why is spiritual certainty praised when it may prevent exploration of deeper truth?
  12. Does the concept of faith override the need for reason and evidence in the pursuit of truth?
  13. How can one differentiate between true divine inspiration and strong personal conviction?
  14. Is believing something without sufficient evidence considered a virtue or a flaw?
  15. Can we be held morally accountable for beliefs shaped by culture, geography, or trauma?
  16. If a just God values truth, why not equip everyone with the same access to knowledge?
